Netiquette literally is "net etiquette" and encompasses the arena of manners on the network/web and in this course. You can find lots of examples online, and read up in depth on the topic on sites like this.

Here is a great article about student emails: https://www.insidehighered.com/views/2015/04/16/advice-students-so-they-dont-sound-silly-emails-essay

For purposes of this class, here are some guidelines:

When emailing anyone (including your instructor) include a MEANINGFUL subject line, a salutation, good grammar and sentence structure and punctuation, and a signature. Here is a good example followed by a bad example:

GOOD

SUBJECT: Confusion about actual due dates to each module in WEB 115.

Dear Mr. von Briesen,

I noticed that each part of moodle has dates on it but i don't see due dates anywhere. Can you clarify when we actually have to get work turned in?

Thanks,

Ashley

BAD

SUBJECT: re  (or) Ashley Wilkes (or) homework (or) confusion

When is our work due each week because i turned something in and it hasn't been graded and i'm really concerned because i have to keep a good grade to keep my financial aid and i had some trouble with understanding the course because moodle is new to me please help.

The last email doesn't let me know what course the student is in, what the subject is, and is not really clear about what the student needs or wants from me. Don't be that person.

When in doubt, ask yourself if what you've done/written is clear and polite, and includes as much information as you can reasonably provide to help the recipient help you. If you can answer yes to these things, you should be fine.
