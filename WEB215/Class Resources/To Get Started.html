The work for this course generally takes the form of some orientation/getting started material, including:

READ & REVIEW:

  • Poke around the whole course to get a feel of the module/material/submodule organization, paying particular attention to the START HERE and the RESOURCES sections.
  • Make sure you know where announcements are and read what you see there.
  • Read any general discussion forum posts - this forum acts like a physical classroom - a place to chat, tell a joke, ask a question, or help someone out. 
  • Review the syllabus and make sure you know what book/materials you need to get

PERFORM THESE ACTIVITIES:

  • Make arrangements to get your books/materials as needed
  • Set up your profile and do the introduction assignment (see the discussions and the links to instructions there)
  • Set up your webspace for this course
  • Create your course contract for this course and linking it to your introduction (below)
  • Create your introduction webpage within your webspace and linking it back to the contract
  • Set up outside accounts (i.e. github, codecademy, freecodecamp, LinkedIn Learning)
  • Make your first blog/journal entry
  • Make your first discussion forum post
